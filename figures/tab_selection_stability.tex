\caption{\textbf{Selection stability across seeds.} For each method we report,
\emph{across 3 seeds}, the mean ($\pm$ std) of \textbf{Active Dims} and
\textbf{Active Blocks}, and three Jaccard agreement scores:
$J_{\text{fam}}$ over the set of \emph{embedding families} kept by each seed
(out of 6 candidates, or 4 for H-ACE);
$J_{\text{blk}}$ over the set of \emph{block indices} selected;
and $J_{\text{dim}}$ over the \emph{actual selected dimensions}, obtained by
replaying the per-seed random permutation that splits each embedding into
blocks. The last column ($\Delta$) is the gap between $J_{\text{dim}}$ and the
expected Jaccard of two uniformly random subsets of the same size; values
near zero indicate that the controller's dimension-level selection is
\emph{statistically indistinguishable} from random, evidencing
massive functional redundancy at the sub-embedding level.}
